Voice Controlled Website

UX Research, UX & UI Design, User Testing

Just Speak Guitar Lessons

Just Speak Guitar Lessons is a Voice navigable website I Researched and Developed whilst at University, presented it as part of my Dissertation aad achieved my First Class Degree with it too. Inspired by a UX meeting focusing on the Advancement of Voice technology I discovered that navigating a website or application vocally was an unexplored paradigm for the Web. Loving a challenge, I set my self the task to Measure The Feasibility of Vocally Navigating a Website using a non-AI voice Controlled Algorithm. This case study tells the story on how I answered this using the button below, you can view the complete site.

Just Speak Guitar Lessons

Case Summary

Product Vision

The Research and Development vision was to ascertain whether there was any demand from the general public. To answer this a project plan was formed on in preparation to calculate a the Feasibility of Vocally Navigating a Rich Content Website :

  • Focus Group to discover the Users needs from the site.
  • Desk Research on similar sites providing the same service.
  • Rapid Prototyping to produce and test multiple iterations of the designs.
  • Transfer Paper Prototypes to Hi Def Designs and test again.
  • Develop the Website.
  • Test the site on Users.

Discoveries

User Testing and Focus Groups were formed by recruiting willing participating students from different departments on the University Campus - this was to create an unbiased set of results.
The realisation that the Survey would have to go to the general public and not just to students on the University campus occurred.
User input was obtained through Public Surveys and Interviews, always ensuring total anonymity for the participants this was to ensure the procedures were ethically upheld.
Realisation of the many accents amongst students, creating the keywords that the User's would use would have to be surveyed and the majority of winning keywords would be used during development.
Think Aloud Protocol was applied on the completed designs and again on the finished product to capture the user's needs, that the focus group didn't manage to capture.

Outcomes

Tasked with designing and developing a Voice Navigable website to answer the Feasibility of such, understanding that every section had to be data-driven in order to answer it without a margin of error.
Results from the Focus Groups formed at the beginning, revealed that there was a need for such a Website and especially in this type of domain. The created online Public Surveys proved fruitful with 106 participant's valued input, improved the design's, produced the keywords for development. The results from the Think Aloud Protocol Tests gave excellent input for Design Improvements Development progression. The Quantitative data from the Surveys and Qualitative Data from the User Interviews were triangulated to reveal if the overarching aim was achieved or not. Project planning was calculated an ETA of 4months to complete, suing the Agile Methodology due to the multiple iterations required during each phase.

Discovery and Ideation

Focus group meeting, consisting of 6 participants, with ages ranging from 18 to 58 years of age, each studying completely different courses at the University. This meeting discovered there was certainly a potential for a trend to develop from this type of Website, in this type of an educational setting (guitar tutorial). There was skepticism involved when the suggestion was made that the project should be a completely stand alone, voice activated website. It was concluded that it should be a website navigable in the usual way using a peripheral and voice controlled too. It was also revealed that the Tutorial, should act and behave as expected, however, should also be less verbose, high definition imagery/ videos to be included with a simplistic HCI.
The data allowed me to paint a clearer picture of what the User's wanted from the product, what they expected and more importantly an accurate representation on who wanted the application. This information provided me with enough information to build a SWOT Analysis and User personas for further research.

What followed were efforts to map the existing user experiences to identify other opportunity developments:

Discovery Analysis

Desk Research

A Desk Study study on similar Tutorials available today occurred to identify design patterns and natural User Flow. so that the new site, although unique to the Web Industry, would be familiar to the user too. It was discovered that none of the sites had Voice Control, one had a Guitar Tuner that used the microphone. It was decided at this point not to include this type od Guitar tuner for this site as it could be catastrophic to the reason that this site will use the microphone. This became a recommendation for future research and development though.

The Javascript libraries found that had some capable Speech Recognition suitable for this website were:
  • Artyom.js
  • Anyang.js
  • Voix.js
  • PocketSphinx.js

After testing the capabilities of these libraries a decision was made to use Annyang.js; it was the smallest library available, had no AI technology and was the cut above the rest in relation to responsiveness, speed and adaptation in speech recognition in comparison with the others identified.

Idea Prioritisation

Data collected from the User Research and Discovery phase produced User expectations and Future Development Opportunities to include for the website. This data along with expectations for the website design advanced Ideation into Hypothesis and Idea Prioritisation.

Based on discovery research findings I came up with three hypothesis to ensure successful Usability, Accessability and Navigation. These covered:

  • Easily Identifiable Navigation areas.
  • Visual hierarchy for the lesson structure on the Home Page.
  • Simple HCI, utilisiation of the unique use of white space to enhance engagement and stop cognitive overload.
  • A visually pleasing and interactive website should increase Customer Retainability.
  • Selective color pallette to increase positive psychology.

The features were listed and mapped based in technical complexity, potential Business and User efficiency values. The highest impact features were expected to be included in the developed product.

Prototyping

Concentrating on high impact feature sets allowed me to create a story that could be split into a few major user scenarios: Product Identity, simplistic HCI and User familiarity that was inspired by the product itself and further intrigue from the competitive research, during the Desk Research. Focusing on the natural User Flow, the Usability and Accessibility of the website, whilst keeping the focus on answering the overarching aim, the following steps in designing the website followed.

Competitive Research

After researching other similar websites, a natural user flow and design pattern was discovered along with page hierarchy that was identical for each site. The sites researched had in excess of 30+ pages, it was decided that having so many pages for this site was not required for testing reasons.

Website Hierarchy Diagram

Data recorded during User Interviews and the public Surveys enabled me to capture and encapsulate the users feelings towards the products tested and through the data recorded during the interviews, now in Persona's, I was able to produce an early Natural User Flow theory through the Website's Hierarchy.

Rapid Prototyping

During Rapid Prototyping, several design iterations that were generated during a short period of time, the features were created and tested in an attempt to optimize characteristics like shape, size, and overall usability based on user feedback and analysis. Once the Rapid Prototyping had ended, the final sketched were transferred to the wireframes, using Adobe XD.

Hi-Def Designs

Once the final tested iteration of the wireframes were designed, all features and functionality was transferred to Hi-Def Designs using Adobe XD, Illustrator and Photoshop for the icons. These were tested using the Think Aloud Protocol and interviews. Pain points were found, errors were caught and rectified which resulting with the completed final designs.

Validation

The prototype was developed using HTML5 for the structure, CSS3 to match the design specs, JQuery and Javascript for speech recognition and to enhance the dynamic content. It was tested on 15 users (5 for each of the created persona's), in order to to validate my Hypothesis. User's paralinguistic features were recorded using a web-cam and video recording software at the agreement of the participant, they were tested ethically a new set of participants were brought in for the second wave of testing. The identity of the participants shall remain anonymised as agreed.

From the previously conducted survey, willing participants tested the prototype created for mobile and desktop viewport's using the Think Aloud Protocol. Asking a series of open ended questions the test administrator was able to understand how the user's felt about the product whilst also testing the age ranges specified earlier to expand the customer base.

First Pass - Designs and Development.

  • The early prototypes and User Behaviour proved that both Speech Navigation and the Usual method were required to enhance Usability.
  • Natural Speech Commands were tested vs Dictated Speech Commands. The keywords found during the Survey were found to be sufficient. Although users could access a table of pre-determined commands at any time no navigate successfully, 95% of the keywords were guessed when testing.
  • Early designs were shown to have too much text content which delayed the user from efficiently moving through to complete tasks, so as requested more imagery and videos were added and verbose removed.
  • Color Psychology, Emphasis on in page keywords such as, "Well done" or encouraging quotes increased a positive emotion.
  • 60% of users found the, in page navigation (index page) on the desktop confusing so redesigning the structure in page navigation (not the top bar) became compulsory.
  • A/B testing on the in page navigation section followed, one was the original design and the other was the restructured design.

Final Pass - Tested Prototype.

All Feedback was analysed, errors were caught and designs improved greatly thanks to this. I observed almost entirely positive feedback from the final designs.

  • Users found the new in page navigation on the index page much easier to follow.
  • Analysis shown that the majority of the users interacted and spent more time using the Speech Navigation as opposed to the usual clickable navigation.
  • All participants were able to complete the task of Vocally navigating from the index page, to the last page and back home. The final test of Vocally navigating from the Home Page, to a page and any part of that page they selected, then returning back to the Home page successfully.
  • During testing it was discovered that 72% of the participants successfully completed the tasks without the need to view the pre-determined command table. Proving the Survey to find natural keywords were a success.
  • 99% of the users were not showing signs of confusion or stress whilst completing the test for the final version of each prototype.

Outcomes

Based on the findings from both the UX research and the Design/ Development validation sessions I was able to produce a list of changes that were addressed and updated in the prototype along with any potential future alterations should a revisit to develop the keywords to enhance the Voice Navigation further in the future.
Triangulating the Quantitative data from the Surveys and Qualitative Data from the User Interviews during and after the Think Aloud Protocol testing, considering that the number of users participating versus users successfully completing the tasks was in the higher percentile - and at a ratio of 5:4 evaluated that the project was a complete success and answered the overarching aim of Measuring the Feasibility of Navigating A Rich Content Website Vocally Using A Non AI Algorithm.
